CARD GAME FOR GRIEF

A card conversation activity to bring family, friends, and those in mourning
together during times of grief.

———

The Context

The students in this design class are domain experts. PhDs in engineering, education, medicine learning how to turn deep technical knowledge into products that serve everyday people. The gap was that building prototypes typically requires fluency in tools like Figma, web development, or other software these students hadn't needed before. They were working with pen, paper, and scissors while a growing landscape of AI tools could let them prototype at much higher fidelity. They just needed to know which tools existed, which ones fit their phase of work, and how to actually use them.

The Approach

Working solo from a mentor's brief, I mapped the AI tool landscape onto the three-phase design process students were already following: research, prototyping, and MVP development. I built a first version, tested it with 12 students, and iterated on their feedback, adding search functionality and specific tools they requested.

The Results

Users rated navigation 4.4/5 and content helpfulness 4.2/5. Feedback pointed toward wanting more depth: example projects, concrete use cases, and tighter workflow integration. A clear roadmap for version 2 if the project continues.
